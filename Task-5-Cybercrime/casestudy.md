Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
Crime Type: UPI Phishing & Social Engineering Fraud
What is it?
UPI fraud is a category of financial cybercrime wherein a fraudster convinces a victim to authorize a money transfer or divulge critical information such as the UPI PIN or OTP on false pretences. Unlike bank frauds, UPI transactions are instantaneous and irreversible, making it impossible to recover the money or information once a fraud has taken place.

How it happens: Step-by-Step
1. Targeting: The fraudster selects a target/victim who has placed a "selling" ad on OLX, Quikr, or a college WhatsApp group.
2. First Contact: The fraudster establishes a connection with the target/victim by posing as a buyer over a call or chat.
3. The Trap: The fraudster sends a "collect request" on BHIM/GPay/PhonePe to the target/victim and says: "I've sent ₹5,000 – just approve the request to receive it."
4. Deception: The target/victim believes they are receiving money and therefore provides their UPI PIN.
5. Theft: The PIN is used to authorize the "collect request," and money is immediately "debited" from the target/victim's account.
6. Vanishing Act: The fraudster immediately goes offline; his/her number is a temporary SIM.

Who Is Targeted?
- College students selling second-hand gadgets, books, or bikes online
- First-time UPI users not aware of the concept of collect and pay
- Job aspirants getting fake requests for 'refund of registration fees'



Consequences
- Financial: Loss of ₹500 to ₹50,000+ with no scope of reversal
- Psychological: Anxiety, shame, and fear of using digital payments
- Legal (for the victim): Time-consuming complaint system with no guarantee of recovery
