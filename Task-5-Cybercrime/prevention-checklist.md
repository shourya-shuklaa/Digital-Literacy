Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
For College Students in India | Digital Ambassador Programme

General Online Safety
1. Use strong and unique passwords for all your accounts. Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use the same password for more than one account.

2. Use two-factor authentication for your important accounts like email, social media, bank apps, and your college portal. Use an authenticator app instead of SMS-based OTP for two-factor authentication whenever possible.

3. Don’t fall for clicking on suspicious links in any email or social media messages or even in the messages you receive on your mobile phones via WhatsApp or SMS. Be cautious of messages claiming prizes, job opportunities, or government schemes, even if they are sent by a government department or a company.

4. Check your app permissions regularly and uninstall any apps you don’t use. Be cautious while giving access to your camera, microphone, or contacts to any app unless it really needs it.

5. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for your important work or for any kind of online transactions or for logging in to your accounts like your bank account or your college portal.

6. Lock your screens whenever you are away from your devices, especially when you are in a library or a canteen or a computer lab.


Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)
7. NEVER enter your UPI PIN for receiving money. UPI PIN is needed only when you are sending or paying someone –not when you are receiving money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN for receiving money, it is 100% a fraud case, Disconnect immediately.

8. Verify collect requests before accepting them. A "collect request" on your GPay/PhonePe/BHIM app means someone is asking you to pay them, read the request carefully before entering your PIN. Scammers often pose as someone who is asking for money that they say you need to pay.

9. Don't share your OTP or Card Details over a call or chat – it's a fraud, no bank or government body will ever ask for your OTP or Card Details. Hang up immediately if someone asks for your Card Details or your One-Time Password.

10. Set a daily UPI transaction limit for your bank app- Set a small limit for daily transactions say Rs. 2,000 to Rs. 5,000 and increase it when you are planning a large transaction

Identity & Social Media Safety
11. Audit your social media privacy settings- Every semester. Make sure that posts are set to "Friends Only." Do not share your phone number, birth date, home address, or college roll number. This information will be used by criminals to impersonate you and get access to your passwords.

12. Reverse-image search profile pictures of people you meet online before sharing any information- Catfishing and romance scams are common on Instagram and Telegram.


Report Cybercrime Immediately
If you or someone you know has become a victim of any of these cybercrimes:
National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal |cybercrime.gov.in|
Cyber Crime Helpline | 1930 (available 24×7 across India) |
Local Police Cyber Cell | File a written complaint at your nearest Police Station |
Bank Fraud Helpline | Immediately call your bank to freeze the transaction |

 Speed matters. Report UPI/Bank fraud within 24 hours – Early reporting has a higher chance of freezing fraud transactions.
